About AIS MUN

Our Mission

The Association International School Model United Nations (AIS MUN) was established with the goal of promoting global awareness and developing diplomatic skills among youth.

Our MUN program provides students with a platform to debate global issues, develop public speaking skills, and engage in meaningful discussions that mirror the real United Nations processes.

We aim to host conferences that attract hundreds of students from international schools across the country, creating a diverse and enriching experience for all participants.

Committees and Topics

WHO

World Health Organization (WHO)

Ensuring Good Health and Well-being through equity and accessibility to medication for all.

ECOSOC

United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)

Ensuring inclusivity and dignity for the Afghan female: The role of education in breaking cycles of ignorance and repression.

UNHCR

United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR)

Promoting tolerance and peaceful co-existence between indigenes and refugee/migrant communities – the need for a new direction and fresh ideas.

UNHRC

United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC)

Peace, Justice and Strong institutions: the role of Civil Society organizations and citizens in enforcing human dignity and equality before the law.

DISEC

Disarmament and International Security Committee (DISEC)

Curbing the new arms race: the need for stricter international regime to foster global peace and security.

FAO

Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)

Zero Hunger and Responsible consumption: Striking a balance in ensuring food security and a sustainable future.

WTO

World Trade Organization (WTO)

Resetting and ensuring equity in Global trade.

ILO

International Labour Organization (ILO)

Combating global human trafficking and forced labor to attain decent work and economic growth.

BRICS

BRICS

Counterbalancing the existing unipolar global order through fresh and bold initiatives in an increasingly polarized international system.

IOM

International Organization for Migration (IOM)

Combatting growing illegal and unregulated international migration by reducing inequality: The role of governments.

AU

African Union Committee (AU)

Ensuring equal opportunities for all Africans: Building climate change proof farming systems and facilitating access to economic resources, as well as appropriate new technology to combat brain-drain and illegal migration.

UNICEF

United Nations Children’s Education Fund (UNICEF)

Building the future we want, developing new funding modules to fund equal and quality access to education for all children.
